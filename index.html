Sign Rules

These are the main patterns to remember.

Positive × Positive = Positive

Same sign for multiplication? Positive result when both are positive.

Negative × Negative = Positive

Two negatives multiply to a positive. Memory trick: “two flips make it face forward again.”

Negative × Positive = Negative

Different signs in multiplication give a negative answer.

Adding same signs

Add the amounts and keep the sign. Example: -4 + -3 = -7.

Adding different signs

Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger one, then keep the sign of the larger number.

Helpful idea

Treat a negative as owing money. Owing more makes you more negative. Paying off debt moves you toward positive.

Tip: for addition, look at whether the numbers are working together or canceling each other out. For multiplication, only focus on whether the signs match.
